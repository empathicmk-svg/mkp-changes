Why this exists

You book the call. You show up. You spend the first forty minutes explaining who you are, what you do, and why you're stuck — and then the clock runs out right as it gets good. You walk away with notes you'll never open and the same fog you came in with.

That's not a bad session. That's an unprepared one.

The clients who get the most out of a strategy session do the thinking before the call. They arrive knowing their own situation cold, so the hour goes to the part only an outside brain can do: seeing what they can't, and deciding what to do about it.

This is that pre-work. It's the worksheet I send before The Session — the questions that surface the real problem so we don't waste the hour finding it. Run it solo before any strategy call, with any consultant. Or run it on yourself when you can't afford one yet.

Set aside ninety minutes. Write your answers down — actually written, not "thought about." The friction of writing is where the clarity lives.

Part 1 — The Identity & Positioning Self-Audit

You can't position what you can't name. Start here. Answer fast and honest; you can refine later.

WHO I ACTUALLY AM (not the title)


  I help ___________________ (specific people)


  do/get __________________ (specific outcome)


  so they can _____________ (the deeper why).



  The thing I'm better at than most people in my field: __________



  The thing people consistently come to ME for: __________



  What I'm known for right now (be honest, even if you hate it): ____



  What I WANT to be known for: __________



  The gap between those two: __________

Worked example

  I help mid-career engineers who want to move into management

  do it without faking a personality they don't have,


  so they can lead without burning out pretending.



  Better at than most: translating technical work into plain stakes.


  People come to me for: untangling messy org problems.


  Known for now: "the reliable senior IC who fixes things."


  Want to be known for: "the one who can run the team."


  The gap: everyone trusts my hands, no one's seen me lead.

Notice what the example surfaces: the problem isn't skill, it's perception. That gap line is gold. It's the thing the session should be about. Find yours.

Part 2 — The Brand Clarity Questions

These are the questions I'd ask in the first fifteen minutes — so you arrive having already answered them. Don't skip the uncomfortable ones. Those are the load-bearing ones.

The Twelve

  • [ ] 1. If a stranger read everything you've published, what would they think you do? Is that right?
  • [ ] 2. What do you say when someone asks "so what do you do?" — and do you cringe when you say it?
  • [ ] 3. Who are the three people whose attention or respect you actually want? Name them.
  • [ ] 4. What do you want them to think, feel, or do after encountering you?
  • [ ] 5. What's the most impressive thing you've done that you've never put anywhere?
  • [ ] 6. What are you tired of explaining? (That's often your real expertise.)
  • [ ] 7. What do you turn down? (Your no's define your brand more than your yes's.)
  • [ ] 8. Where are you currently being underestimated or misread?
  • [ ] 9. What would you say if you weren't worried about sounding arrogant?
  • [ ] 10. What's the one sentence you wish people understood about your work?
  • [ ] 11. What's working right now that you can't explain — and want to do more of on purpose?
  • [ ] 12. If nothing changed in twelve months, what would you most regret?

Write a real answer to each. One sentence minimum. The session moves at the speed of the answers you already have.

Part 3 — The Goal-Setting Framework for a 60-Minute Session

An hour is short. The single biggest waste is showing up with a vague goal — "I want clarity on my brand." Clarity on what, decided how, by when? Tighten it.

Step 1: Name the one decision

A session should produce a decision, not a feeling. Finish this sentence:

  By the end of this hour, I want to have decided: _______________


  EXAMPLE: "...whether to reposition as a leadership coach or stay


  a technical consultant — and what my LinkedIn headline becomes


  either way."

If you have three things you want to decide, rank them. You'll likely only get through one well. Better one decision made than three half-touched.

Step 2: Define "useful"

  This session was worth it if I leave with: _____________________


  Pick concrete outputs, e.g.:


  - A positioning statement I can actually use


  - A clear next 3 actions


  - A decision I've been avoiding, made


  - A rewritten headline / bio / pitch

Step 3: Surface the blockers

What's actually stopping you? Name it so the session can hit it directly.

  What I think is blocking me: __________

  What might REALLY be blocking me (the scary version): __________


  What I've already tried that didn't work: __________

Step 4: The 3-2-1 prep

  3 things I want feedback on: ___, ___, ___

  2 questions I'm afraid to ask out loud: ___, ___


  1 outcome that would make this a no-brainer to have done: ___

Worked example — filled out

  Decision: Whether to lead my consulting with "strategy" or "writing."


  Useful if I leave with: one clear positioning line + my new


  homepage headline + a yes/no on dropping the lowest-tier offer.



  Think is blocking me: I don't know which is more marketable.


  REALLY blocking me: I'm scared "writer" sounds less senior than


  "strategist" and I'll be paid less.


  Already tried: rewriting my bio four times, kept both, said neither.



  3 for feedback: homepage headline, my pricing page, my pitch on calls


  2 scary questions: Am I charging too little? Is my niche too narrow?


  1 no-brainer outcome: walk away able to say what I do in one sentence.

Look at how much sharper this is than "help me with my brand." A session built on this prep can go straight to the real fear in minute two — and spend the other fifty-eight solving it.

The pre-session checklist

Do this the morning of the call.

  • [ ] All three parts filled in — written, not thought
  • [ ] The one decision named in a single sentence
  • [ ] "Useful" defined as concrete outputs
  • [ ] The scary version of the blocker written down
  • [ ] Relevant links/copy gathered in one place (bio, site, profile)
  • [ ] Phone off, notes app open, somewhere you can talk freely
  • [ ] One question you'd be slightly embarrassed to ask — bring it anyway

After: lock it in within 24 hours

The session's value evaporates if you don't capture it fast. Within a day:

  THE ONE DECISION I MADE: __________

  THE NEXT 3 ACTIONS (with dates):


    1. __________ by ___


    2. __________ by ___


    3. __________ by ___


  THE SENTENCE I'M KEEPING: __________


  WHAT I STILL NEED TO RESOLVE: __________
