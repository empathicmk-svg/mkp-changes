The LinkedIn Bio Fix Kit

Fix the first thing everyone reads about you — in the next fifteen minutes.

By MK Parrish · $9

Your LinkedIn headline and About section are the most-read words you'll ever write — and for most people, they're doing nothing. They list a job title, a few buzzwords, and a "passionate about" or two, then quietly lose the exact people who were about to reach out.

This kit fixes that fast. No personal-branding philosophy, no 40-page workbook. Just the formula, the templates, and the swipe lines to rewrite your headline and About today. Fill in the blanks, paste it in, done.

Your bio isn't a résumé. It's the answer to one silent question: "Is this person for me?"

The 60-Second Diagnosis

Open your profile and check the honest truth. You need this kit if any of these are true:

  • Your headline is just your job title (or worse, "Open to work").
  • Your About section opens with "I am a [role] with X years of experience…"
  • You use any of: passionate, results-driven, seasoned, proven track record, detail-oriented, wearing many hats, journey, guru, ninja.
  • A stranger could not tell, in five seconds, who you help and what changes for them.
  • It reads like it was written for a hiring algorithm, not to a human.

If you flinched at even one, keep reading. The fixes are below.

The Headline Formula

Your headline follows you everywhere — every comment, every search result, every connection request. It has one job: make the right person think "that's exactly who I need."

Use one of these three structures. Fill in the brackets.

Formula A — Who + Outcome (best for services & consulting)

I help [WHO] [DO THE THING] without [THE PAIN] | [CREDIBILITY]

Example: "I help female founders rewrite their messaging so it finally sounds like them | Ex-agency, 200+ brands"

Formula B — Role + Proof + Edge (best for job seekers)

[ROLE] who [SPECIFIC RESULT] · [INDUSTRY/NICHE] · [WHAT MAKES YOU DIFFERENT]

Example: "Product marketer who turned a flat funnel into 40% MoM growth · B2B SaaS · I write the copy and read the data"

Formula C — The Stand (best for creators & founders)

[WHAT YOU BELIEVE / BUILD]. [WHO IT'S FOR]. [THE PROOF OR PROMISE].

Example: "Websites that sound like a person, not a press release. For founders relaunching. From $6K."

Rules: Lead with the reader's outcome, not your title. One specific number or proof beats five adjectives. If a competitor could paste your headline onto their own profile unchanged, it's too generic — make it yours.

The About Section Template

The first two lines are everything — they're all that shows before "…see more." Earn the click, then keep it human.

Copy this skeleton and fill it in:

[HOOK — one or two lines that name the reader's problem or a sharp belief.

Not "I am a…". Make them feel seen or make them curious.]



[WHAT YOU DO — one plain sentence. Who you help, and what changes for them.]



[PROOF — one or two concrete results, names, or specifics. No vague


"track record." Show, don't claim.]



[HOW YOU WORK / WHAT MAKES YOU DIFFERENT — the thing only you would say.


Your actual point of view.]



[ONE HUMAN LINE — something true and a little personal. People hire people.]



[CALL TO ACTION — low pressure. "DM me 'AUDIT' and I'll take a look,"


or "Booking [MONTH] now — link below."]

Worked example (before → after):

Before: "Experienced marketing professional with a passion for driving results and a proven track record across multiple industries. Skilled in strategy, content, and brand."

After: "Most founders' websites read like a brochure nobody asked for. I rewrite them so they sound like a person and actually sell. I've rewritten messaging for 200+ brands — the ones that finally booked calls had one thing in common: they stopped trying to sound impressive and started sounding true. I write the strategy and the words. Currently booking two builds for [MONTH]. DM me 'SITE' to see if it's a fit."

See the difference? The "after" never says "passionate." It shows a point of view, one proof point, and a way in.

The Swipe File: 15 Opening Lines That Earn the Click

Steal these for your first line. Adapt the brackets.

  1. Most [audience] are one rewrite away from [outcome]. Here's what I fix.
  2. Your [thing] isn't broken. Your positioning is.
  3. I've [done specific thing] [number] times. Here's the pattern nobody talks about.
  4. "[Common objection]" — said every client right before [result].
  5. The best [role] don't [common mistake]. They [the better move].
  6. I help [who] stop sounding like everyone else.
  7. [Number] years, [number] [clients/projects], one obsession: [your thing].
  8. If your [asset] sounds like a hostage statement, we should talk.
  9. I do one thing extremely well: [the thing].
  10. Hiring managers / clients skim. I write so they stop.
  11. The gap between talented and booked is usually words. I close it.
  12. I turned [unglamorous origin] into [credibility]. Here's how I help you.
  13. Not a guru. Not a ninja. Just very good at [specific].
  14. You don't need more content. You need to sound like you. Start here.
  15. Quietly excellent at [thing]. Loudly allergic to jargon.

The Banned-Words List

Delete these on sight. Each one is a place where you told instead of showed:

passionate · results-driven · seasoned · proven track record · detail-oriented · think outside the box · wear many hats · synergy · leverage (as a verb) · journey · guru · ninja · rockstar · thought leader (about yourself) · dynamic · go-getter · hardworking · team player · self-starter · unlock · empower · elevate.

For every one you cut, replace it with the specific thing it was hiding. "Results-driven" → the actual result. "Detail-oriented" → the mistake you catch that others miss.

The 15-Minute Fix Checklist

Do it in order. Set a timer.

  1. Rewrite the headline using Formula A, B, or C. (4 min)
  2. Write two hook lines for your About from the swipe file. Pick the better one. (3 min)
  3. Fill the About template — one sentence per block. (5 min)
  4. Run the banned-words scan. Delete and replace each one. (2 min)
  5. Add the CTA. Tell people exactly what to do next. (1 min)
  6. Read it out loud. If you wouldn't say it to someone's face, rewrite that line.

That's it. You just fixed the first thing everyone reads about you.
