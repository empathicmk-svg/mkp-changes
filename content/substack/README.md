# The Margins — Substack content & monetization plan

This folder contains every piece of writing from the website (`/writing` and `/posts`), rewritten as clean, ready-to-publish Substack drafts. Substack has no public API for creating posts, so these can't be pushed into your account automatically — but each file is formatted so you can **copy the body straight into the Substack editor** and hit publish.

## How to publish each one (2 minutes each)

1. In Substack: **Dashboard → New post → Text post.**
2. Copy the **title** and **subtitle** from the file's front-matter into the title/subtitle fields.
3. Copy everything **below the front-matter** (`---`) into the body. Markdown headings (`##`), bold, lists, and blockquotes paste cleanly into Substack's editor.
4. For the paid essay (`02`), place the paywall where the file says `SUGGESTED PAYWALL HERE` — in the editor, type `/paywall` on that line.
5. Set the audience (Free / Paid) per the table above, then publish or schedule.

> Tip: publish the three **free** posts first to seed the publication, then publish the **paid** essay so the very first thing a new subscriber sees is a reason to upgrade.

## Recommended monetization model (now live on the site)

Patreon-style multi-tier pricing doesn't map to how Substack actually works. Substack monetizes through **one free tier, one paid subscription (monthly + annual), and an optional Founding Member price.** The site's subscription section has been updated to match:

| Level | Price | What it is |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **The Free List** | Free | Public essays, the poetry, and a monthly strategy note. Grows the email list — the real asset. |
| **The Margins** | **$9/mo or $90/yr** | The full archive, weekly frameworks pulled from client work, positioning teardowns, and voice & copy templates. |
| **Founding Member** | **$300/yr** | Everything in The Margins, plus quarterly live office hours, direct-message access, and priority feedback on your own copy. |

### Set this up in Substack

- **Settings → Payments:** turn on paid subscriptions. Set monthly to **$9**, annual to **$90** (a built-in ~17% incentive to pay yearly).
- **Founding Member:** enable it and set the suggested amount to **$300/yr**. Add the perks above to the description.
- **Welcome email:** point new free subscribers to The Rewrite poem and the AI essay, then make the soft case for upgrading.
- **Paywall cadence:** keep ~70% of posts free to grow the list; reserve the frameworks, teardowns, and templates for paid. The free essays do the selling.

## Why these access levels

- **Free essays + poem + quotes** are your top-of-funnel. They're the proof of voice that makes someone trust you enough to subscribe — and eventually pay.
- **The conversion-math essay is paywalled after a strong free preview.** It's the most directly commercial piece, so it both demonstrates the paid value and gives free readers a concrete reason to upgrade.
