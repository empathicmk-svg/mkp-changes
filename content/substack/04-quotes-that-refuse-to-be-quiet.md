A living collection of words worth keeping — from the people who put language around things most of us only feel. If something here stays with you, that's the point.

---

> You are not accidental. The world could not have happened without you.
> **— Rainer Maria Rilke**, *Letters to a Young Poet* · Identity

> If you ask me what I came to do in this world, I, an artist, will answer you: I am here to live out loud.
> **— Émile Zola** · Voice

> You have to decide what your highest priorities are and have the courage — pleasantly, smilingly, non-apologetically — to say no to other things. And the way you do that is by having a bigger yes burning inside.
> **— Stephen Covey**, *The 7 Habits of Highly Effective People* · Boundaries

> The only way out of the labyrinth of suffering is to forgive.
> **— John Green**, *Looking for Alaska* · Healing

> I am not afraid of storms, for I am learning how to sail my ship.
> **— Louisa May Alcott**, *Little Women* · Courage

> Darkness cannot drive out darkness; only light can do that. Hate cannot drive out hate; only love can do that.
> **— Martin Luther King Jr.**, *Strength to Love* · Faith

> One is not born, but rather becomes, a woman.
> **— Simone de Beauvoir**, *The Second Sex* · Identity

> The most common form of despair is not being who you are.
> **— Søren Kierkegaard** · Authenticity

> You were made for a world within a world within a world — and none of them require your apology.
> **— Howard Thurman**, *Jesus and the Disinherited* · Liberation

> You are allowed to be both a masterpiece and a work in progress simultaneously.
> **— Sophia Bush** · Growth

> We are called to be architects of the future, not its victims.
> **— Buckminster Fuller** · Reinvention

> Until the lion learns to write, every story will glorify the hunter.
> **— African Proverb** · Voice

> Silence is the language of God. All else is poor translation.
> **— Rumi** · Spirituality

> Not everything that is faced can be changed, but nothing can be changed until it is faced.
> **— James Baldwin** · Truth

> To love at all is to be vulnerable. Love anything and your heart will be wrung and possibly broken. If you want to make sure of keeping it intact you must give it to no one, not even an animal.
> **— C.S. Lewis**, *The Four Loves* · Love

> She was a girl who knew how to be happy even when she was sad. And that's important.
> **— Marilyn Monroe** · Resilience

> The function of freedom is to free someone else.
> **— Toni Morrison** · Liberation

> The cave you fear to enter holds the treasure you seek.
> **— Joseph Campbell** · Courage

> The most important kind of freedom is to be what you really are. You trade in your reality for a role.
> **— Jim Morrison** · Authenticity

> I am no longer accepting the things I cannot change. I am changing the things I cannot accept.
> **— Angela Davis** · Reinvention

> Grace is the gift of feeling sure that our future, even our dying, is more presence, not less.
> **— Simone Weil** · Faith

> There is no greater agony than bearing an untold story inside you.
> **— Zora Neale Hurston**, *Dust Tracks on a Road* · Voice

> It is never too late to be what you might have been.
> **— George Eliot** · Reinvention

> The wound is the place where the Light enters you.
> **— Rumi** · Healing

> I am no bird; and no net ensnares me: I am a free human being with an independent will.
> **— Charlotte Brontë**, *Jane Eyre* · Liberation

> The privilege of a lifetime is to become who you truly are.
> **— Carl Jung** · Identity

> I took a deep breath and listened to the old brag of my heart: I am, I am, I am.
> **— Sylvia Plath**, *The Bell Jar* · Identity

> No one can make you feel inferior without your consent.
> **— Eleanor Roosevelt** · Liberation

> It is better to be hated for what you are than to be loved for what you are not.
> **— André Gide** · Authenticity

> Your pain is the breaking of the shell that encloses your understanding.
> **— Khalil Gibran**, *The Prophet* · Healing

> The longest journey is the journey inward.
> **— Dag Hammarskjöld**, *Markings* · Spirituality
