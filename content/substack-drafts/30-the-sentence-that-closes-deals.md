Every deal has a moment where the prospect became certain. Here's what creates that moment.

When I talk to founders about deals that closed well — faster than usual, without much back-and-forth on price — there's almost always a moment they can identify. A specific thing that happened where the prospect went from evaluating to decided.

Sometimes it's the demo. Sometimes it's a reference call. But a surprising number of times, it's a sentence. Something someone read or heard that made the decision obvious.

## What the sentence does

The sentence that closes deals does one thing: it names the specific failure mode of every alternative the prospect has been considering.

Not in a competitive takedown way. In a diagnostic way. It says: here's the specific thing that's wrong with the current approach — and the prospect reads it and thinks, yes, that's exactly what's wrong.

In that moment, they've already chosen you. The deal from that point is administrative.

## Where the sentence lives

It lives in your copy, if you've written it. It lives in your sales collateral, if you've identified it. It lives in the talk track of your best rep, if you've extracted it.

The problem is that most companies haven't identified it explicitly. They know it works in the sales conversation — their closer uses it instinctively — but it hasn't been systematized, it hasn't been put on the homepage, and it hasn't been put in the emails that run before the demo.

So the deals that close fast are the ones who found it. The ones that stall are the ones who haven't heard it yet.

## How to find your sentence

Talk to the deals that closed fastest and ask: was there a moment in our conversation where you knew we were the right choice? What was said?

Listen for the specific claim. Not the feature — the diagnostic statement. The thing that named a failure in the alternative.

Write that down. Put it on your homepage. Put it in the first email after the demo request. Watch what it does to cycle time.
