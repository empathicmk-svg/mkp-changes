I use it every day. Here's the honest account.

AI is good for more than most critics admit and less than most practitioners claim. The honest list is actually quite useful, once you stop trying to make it do the things it can't.

## What it does well

**First-draft scaffolding.** You have a structure in mind. You need 600 words around it. You can write a prompt, get a rough draft, and spend your energy on the editorial layer rather than the blank-page layer. The draft will be mediocre. That's fine. Your editing is where the quality comes from.

**Format conversion.** You wrote 2,000 words of exploratory thinking. You need a 300-word summary, a LinkedIn post, and three email subject lines. AI does this quickly and competently.

**Research synthesis.** You need to understand a client's industry quickly. You can get a working orientation in thirty minutes rather than three hours.

**Light editing.** Tighten this paragraph. Improve the transitions. Make this less wordy. AI is useful here if you've already decided what the paragraph is supposed to say.

**Ideation.** You need twenty angle ideas for a piece you're writing. Generate them, ignore sixteen, develop four. The bottleneck isn't ideas. It's which idea to pursue.

## What it doesn't do

It doesn't have opinions. It has distributions. Ask it to take a strong position and it will simulate one, but the simulation doesn't come from a genuine view — it comes from the statistical center of everything that has been written about that position.

This matters for brand voice. Your brand voice is not the statistical center of your industry's communication norms. It is the specific way you see a specific set of problems differently from everyone else. AI will pull you toward average. The work is to pull away from it.

It also doesn't know what to leave out. Editing is mostly deletion. The judgment about what stays is a judgment about what matters, and that judgment requires a perspective AI doesn't have.

## The ratio

In my work: probably 20% of first-draft output touches AI in some way. 0% of the thinking does.

The thinking is the job. Everything else is production.

---

*The writers and strategists who will still be valuable in five years are the ones developing stronger opinions and sharper judgment. AI raises the floor on execution. It doesn't touch the ceiling.*
