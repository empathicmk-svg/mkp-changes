AI produces drafts. You produce the opinion. Those are not the same thing.

A draft is the arrangement of words. An opinion is the reason those words are arranged that way — the underlying conviction that determines what gets included, what gets cut, and what gets emphasized.

The draft is visible. The opinion isn't. But the opinion is what moves people.

## Why this distinction matters

When you hand a draft to AI to improve, AI can optimize the arrangement. Cleaner sentences. Better transitions. Stronger verb choices. The surface gets better.

What it can't improve is the conviction underneath the surface. If the draft is hedging — if it's trying to say something without fully committing to it — the improved version is a more polished hedge.

The polished hedge is one of the more dangerous outputs in marketing because it sounds confident without being committed. Prospects can feel the lack of conviction even when they can't name it. It reads as corporate. Professional. Inert.

## What an opinion looks like in copy

An opinion takes a position. It says this is the thing and implies that other things are less true.

"Most content strategies fail because companies create for SEO and not for readers" is an opinion. It implies that SEO-first content is the wrong approach. Some readers will disagree. The ones who agree will trust you for saying it plainly.

"Content strategy should align with your audience's needs and your business objectives" is not an opinion. It's a truism. Nobody disagrees with it because it says nothing precise enough to disagree with.

Copy built on opinions converts. Copy built on truisms decorates.

## The practical implication

Before you generate a draft, write your actual opinion in one or two ugly sentences. Don't clean it up. Just write what you think.

Then use that as the input for the draft.

The draft will carry the opinion. Without it, the draft is just words.
