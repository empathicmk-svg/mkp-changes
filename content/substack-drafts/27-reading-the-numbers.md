Good copy starts with the metrics. Here's which ones to look at.

Before I write anything for a client, I want their numbers. Not their goals — their actuals. What is actually happening at each stage of their funnel, and where is the behavior inconsistent with the positioning?

The numbers tell you where the copy is working and where it's failing. Writing without them is guessing.

## The four metrics that matter

**Bounce rate by source.** Not aggregate — by channel. If your paid traffic bounces at 80% and your organic traffic bounces at 40%, your paid ad message is misaligned with your landing page. The promise the ad makes and the promise the page makes are different. This is a copy/channel alignment problem.

**Time on page for key pages.** If the homepage average is under 30 seconds, the first sentence isn't doing its job. If the pricing page average is under 20 seconds, prospects aren't reading — they're looking for a number and leaving when they find it, which means the context around the number isn't doing work.

**Conversion rate by traffic segment.** Different segments convert at different rates. If referral traffic converts at 4% and organic converts at 0.8%, you have a positioning legibility problem — the people who get the context (the referral) convert; the people who arrive cold can't figure out what you are.

**Deal source vs. messaging.** Which channels produce deals that close fastest? Those channels are probably delivering the best-fit prospects. Why? What does that channel do differently in terms of pre-qualifying the prospect? That answer is what your other channels should be saying.

## What you're looking for

You're looking for inconsistencies — places where the behavior is different from what the funnel logic would predict. Inconsistencies tell you where something in the copy or positioning is misaligned with something in the visitor's expectation.

Fix the inconsistency, not the aggregate number.

## The conversation this enables

When I can go into a copy engagement with this data, the first conversation is entirely different. Instead of "what do you want to sound like," we're talking about "here is specifically where visitors are stopping and why."

That's a fixable conversation. "What do you want to sound like" is not.
