Not a tone guide. Not a persona. Not a list of words to use and avoid.

Voice is the residue of someone with an opinion making decisions on the page.

That sentence is worth sitting with for a moment because it changes what you're looking for when you try to develop one.

You're not looking for an aesthetic. You're looking for an opinion. The aesthetic follows.

## Where voice comes from

Voice comes from writing privately and repeatedly about the things that matter to you professionally, until you know what you actually think — not what you're supposed to think, not what sounds authoritative, not what your peers in the industry would nod along to.

What you actually think.

This is harder than it sounds. Most people have never been asked to separate their genuine view from the received wisdom they absorbed in their field. They've absorbed so much of the conventional wisdom that it feels like their own thinking. It isn't.

Voice starts emerging when you're willing to notice where your genuine view and the conventional wisdom diverge. That divergence is the material.

## What distinguishes voice from style

Style is how you construct a sentence. Short or long. Dense or spare. Technical or accessible.

Voice is what the sentence is trying to say.

Two writers can have identical styles and completely different voices. They can write in the same length sentences, with the same rhythm, in the same register — and one of them says something and the other says the average of everything they've read.

Voice is the something. Style is the delivery.

You can hire someone to improve your style. You cannot hire someone to give you a voice. What you can do is hire someone to help you find and articulate the voice that is already there — to strip away the professional varnish and get to the actual point of view underneath.

That's what the best brand voice work does.

## How to find yours

Write for ten minutes about something in your industry that you think is wrong. Not wrong in a small way. Wrong in a way that you find genuinely frustrating.

Don't clean it up. Don't make it diplomatic. Just write what you think.

Read it back.

That roughness — the directness, the specificity, the irritation — is closer to your voice than anything you've written for public consumption. Start there.
