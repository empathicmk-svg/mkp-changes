The tool is pulling toward the mean. Your job is to pull away from it.

Here's the dynamic that most people don't name clearly enough: AI is trained to produce output that is fluent, coherent, and broadly applicable. Those qualities produce output that sounds like everything else. Because everything else is what it was trained on.

You are not trying to sound like everything else. You are trying to sound like yourself — specifically, the version of yourself that has a clear point of view about a specific set of problems. These two goals are in direct tension.

Using AI productively means knowing when to accept its output and when to reject it because it's pulled you toward average.

## The averaging tells

Read AI-generated copy and look for these signals:

**Balanced qualifications** — "While X has benefits, it also has limitations." Real voices don't balance in this way. They pick a side and defend it.

**Industry-standard vocabulary** — "Leverage," "scalable," "streamline," "actionable insights." These words appear because they appear everywhere. They signal nothing.

**Absent antagonist** — Strong positioning implies there's a wrong way to do something. AI-generated copy often has no wrong way. It just describes a right way.

**Generic audience** — "Business leaders" instead of "Series B SaaS founders whose sales cycles are stalling."

When you see these signals, the draft needs your opinion, not more prompting.

## How to re-inject voice

After you get a draft, read it aloud. Mark every sentence that sounds like it could appear in any company's newsletter.

Rewrite those sentences from your genuine perspective. One at a time. Don't ask AI to make them more specific — make them more specific yourself, from your actual view.

The draft becomes the scaffold. The sentences become yours.

## What this requires

It requires that you have a view. That you know what you think about the problem, the category, the client. That you've done the private writing that produces the public clarity.

AI can't give you the view. It can help you express it once you have it. That's the right use of the tool.

---

*The people who are going to do the best work with AI are the ones who already have strong opinions. The ones who don't are going to get very good at producing polished nothing.*
