The difference between The Edit, The Rewrite, and The New Chapter — and how to tell which one is yours.

I offer three tiers of brand messaging work. The tier matters because the right one for your situation isn't always the one you'd assume.

Most companies that think they need a full rebrand need an edit. Most companies that think they need a new tagline need a rewrite. Most companies that think they need copy help need positioning work.

Here's how to figure out which is actually yours.

## The Edit ($250)

You know what you do. You know who you're for. Your positioning is directionally correct. The problem is execution — the copy is wordy, or unclear, or doesn't match the quality of the product.

The Edit sharpens without rebuilding. If the foundation is sound, you don't need a new foundation.

Signs you're here: you can describe what you do in a sentence that's accurate, even if it's not punchy. Your best customers would recognize your positioning statement. The copy just needs to be better.

## The Rewrite ($2,500)

The copy exists but the positioning has drifted, or was never sharp. The company has grown and the words are describing a previous version. You have a sense that something is off but can't name it.

The Rewrite rebuilds the messaging from the positioning level down. We start with what you actually are now, not what the homepage says. Then we write from there.

Signs you're here: you wince when someone reads your homepage aloud. Your current customers would describe you differently than your copy does. There's a gap between what you're proud of and what you're saying.

## The New Chapter (Custom)

The identity itself is in transition. A rebrand, a pivot, a new market, a founder stepping into a new lane. The positioning isn't drifted — it's genuinely uncertain, and the work is figuring out what you are before figuring out how to say it.

This is strategic work first, copy work second. It requires more time because it requires more thinking.

Signs you're here: you're not sure what the right answer is, not just how to say it. The conversation about your positioning has been going in circles. There's more than one version of the company that feels possible.

## The question that narrows it

If I read your current copy and you read it back to me, would you say "that's us, just said better" — or would you say "that's not quite us anymore"?

First answer: The Edit.
Second answer: The Rewrite or The New Chapter, depending on how far off it is.

---

*This is usually the question I ask in the first five minutes of a conversation. The answer tells me almost everything I need to know about where to start.*
