The weeks between discovery and close are not a sales problem. They're a clarity problem.

I've watched founders invest in better sales training, a second AE, a refined demo script — and then wonder why the average cycle is still six months when a competitor is closing in six weeks.

The answer is almost always in the copy. Specifically, in what the copy failed to do before the sales conversation started.

## What the sales cycle actually is

A sales cycle is the time it takes a prospect to become certain that you are the right choice.

Some of that certainty gets built in the sales process. But most of it gets built — or not built — before the first call. On the homepage, in the emails, in the case studies, in the LinkedIn posts that got them curious enough to book the demo.

If that pre-call material didn't do the work, your sales team is doing it manually. That's slow. It's expensive. And it's fragile, because it depends on the rep rather than the system.

## The specific failure

Most B2B copy tells the prospect what the product does. It doesn't tell them why the way they're currently solving this problem is wrong.

That second thing is the thing that creates urgency. Until a prospect understands that their current approach has a specific, costing flaw, there is no urgency. They'll evaluate you thoughtfully and circle back at a convenient time.

Copy that names the flaw in the status quo shortens the cycle because it does the diagnostic work before the prospect arrives. They show up to the call already convinced that something needs to change. The conversation becomes about whether you're the right change, not whether change is necessary.

## The practical fix

Look at your top-of-funnel content — homepage, emails, LinkedIn, whatever you send before the demo.

Does any of it name a specific failure mode in the way most of your prospects currently solve the problem?

Not a category flaw. A specific failure. Something your best customers would recognize and confirm is accurate.

That naming is what shortens cycles.

---

*The fastest-closing engagements I've seen came from companies whose copy had already done the diagnostic work. The sales team was just confirming what the prospect already believed.*
