## 6) Amazon Book Description (~4,000 characters, bold-friendly, no unsupported HTML)

**Your bio is three jobs behind. Your LinkedIn sounds like a committee wrote it.** And somewhere in the gap between who you are and how you read on the page, opportunities are quietly passing you by.

You are not generic. So why does your positioning sound like it?

**Write Yourself Into the Room** is for the founder, executive, consultant, creative, or career changer who has outgrown their own words. You've done the work. You've changed. But the language describing you hasn't caught up — and in rooms you'll never be in, people are deciding who you are based on copy that no longer fits.

This is the book that closes the gap.

MK Parrish doesn't hand you another set of buzzwords or a "personal brand" that sounds like everyone else's vision board. She hands you a method. Sharp, specific, and built for people who would rather be read accurately than read often.

**Inside, you'll learn the Three-Layer Positioning Framework** — a way to write about yourself that holds up under pressure: the layer that earns trust, the layer that creates distinction, and the layer that makes people *remember* you instead of confusing you with the other ten people who do what you do.

**What this book gives you:**

**— The Three-Layer Positioning Framework.** Stop describing your job. Start positioning your judgment, your edge, and your point of view — in language a stranger can repeat.

**— Bio templates that don't sound like templates.** Short, medium, and long versions for your website, your LinkedIn, your speaker intro, and the email that decides whether you get the meeting.

**— A LinkedIn audit checklist.** Go line by line through your headline, About section, and experience — and find every place you're hiding, hedging, or sounding like a press release.

**— A voice-first writing approach.** Most positioning advice flattens you into corporate paste. This teaches you to write in your actual voice — the one people trust in the room — and put it on the page without losing the polish.

**— Language for being read accurately.** The specific words, structures, and moves that make people understand exactly what you do, why it matters, and why you.

This is not a confidence book. It's a *craft* book. You don't need to feel ready — you need the right sentences, in the right order, doing the right work.

**Who this is for:**

- **Founders** whose company story has outgrown their personal story
- **Executives** between titles who need to sound like the next role, not the last one
- **Consultants and coaches** drowning in a sea of identical "I help people" bios
- **Creatives** who can position everyone but themselves
- **Career changers** who need their experience to read as an asset, not an apology

If you've ever stared at a blank "About" field and felt the gap between your real self and your written self — this book is the bridge.

**Wry, exacting, and refreshingly free of fluff,** *Write Yourself Into the Room* treats your words the way a good strategist would: as the difference between being overlooked and being chosen.

The right people can't pick you if they can't *read* you.

**Write the version of yourself that gets you in the room — and pick up your copy today.**

- **No content warnings required.** The book is business/career nonfiction with no sensitive or restricted content.

It may be used descriptively in the description, A+ content, and interior, but **do not** put it in the title, subtitle, or use LinkedIn's logo/branding in the cover or A+ images.
