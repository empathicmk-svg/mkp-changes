### Post 1 — Essay: What AI Can't Write For You

AI does not write badly. It writes confidently average.

Run the same prompt through the same model with two different companies' positioning briefs. You will get two outputs that are remarkable for how similar they are. Both will include "empower," "seamless," "reimagine," and the construction "we don't just X — we Y."

That is not a bug. That is what the model was built to do.

The brands winning attention right now are the ones with a writer at the top of the funnel making specific, opinionated choices. Someone who picks a fight. Who knows what to leave out. Who sounds like a person — not the average of all existing persons.

Three things AI cannot do for your brand:

1. Pick a fight. AI is statistically incapable of disagreeing with the consensus, because the consensus is the training data.
2. Sound like a person. It can produce a passable imitation, but it loses the load-bearing weirdness that makes voice memorable.
3. Know what to leave out. AI defaults to comprehensiveness. Comprehensive copy does not convert.

The full essay is on the site. Eight minutes.

### Post 2 — Essay: The Conversion Math Founders Avoid

When a founder hesitates to spend $5,000 on a brand rewrite, the hesitation is almost always framed as a budget question.

It is not a budget question. It is a math question.

The same founder who won't flinch at $40,000 in quarterly ad spend sent all of that traffic to a homepage they wrote on a Tuesday in the back of a Lyft, three years ago, when they were a different company.

The asymmetry is staggering.

You are paying for the audience. You are paying for the funnel. You are paying for the people. The one piece that sits between every prospect and every conversion — the words on the page — is the cheapest and most-neglected component of the entire stack. And it is the only one with a multiplier effect on everything else.

The quick version of the math:
— Annual visitors × current conversion rate × average client value
— Run it again with the conversion rate 25% higher
— Subtract

That number is what the current copy is costing you per year.

### Post 3 — The Rewrite / Brand Awareness

Most brand strategists never write a word of their own.

I do — poetry, memoir, ghostwritten executive content, brand copy — all from the same voice. The reason my clients say their copy finally sounds like them is not because I follow a framework.

It is because I write constantly. For years, privately, before anyone paid me to do it for them. And that practice is what makes my ear for language precise enough to work with someone else's.

You cannot fake that kind of attunement. You develop it.

A few lines from "The Rewrite," a poem shared by thousands of women in the middle of reinvention:

*I am the author. I always have been.*
*I just had to believe in the plot I was in.*

What you hire when you hire me: the same voice that writes that — pointed at your story.
