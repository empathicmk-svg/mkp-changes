You wrote it. You sat with it. You shipped it. And it's still not landing.

That's not a you problem. That's a draft problem — and a draft is the easiest thing in the world to fix once someone reads it the way your audience does.

The Edit is the fastest way to find out what your copy is actually saying versus what you meant it to say. Send me one piece — a landing page, a bio, a cold email, a pitch, a post that should have hit and didn't — and within 48 hours you get it back rewritten. Not marked up. Not "have you considered." Rewritten. The version you would have written if you weren't standing too close to it.

I read for one thing: where you're being misread. The line that buries the point. The opener that asks for trust before earning it. The qualifier that quietly tells the reader you're not sure. Then I cut, reorder, and sharpen until the piece does the single job you hired it to do.

You don't get decoration. You get precision. Shorter where short wins. Specific where you were vague. Confident where you were hedging. The goal is never to make it sound more like me — it's to make it sound unmistakably like you, on your best day.
