Most people use AI to write faster. Then they wonder why everything they ship sounds like a press release that went to finishing school — smooth, confident, and completely forgettable.

The problem was never the model. It's the prompt. "Write me a bio" gets you the average of every bio on the internet, because that is literally what a language model returns: the middle. Your job is to drag it off the middle and onto *you*.

This vault is the prompt library behind the Services Vault. Every framework in those seven guides — positioning, the rewrite, the bio, website copy, voice capture, content strategy — has a matching set of prompts here. You bring the judgment. The prompts do the typing.

A few rules before you start.

> The prompt is the strategy. A vague prompt is a vague brand.

## How to Use This Vault

Every prompt here is built to be copied, pasted, and customized. They use a simple system.

**The brackets.** Anything in `[SQUARE BRACKETS]` is a blank you fill in. Replace `[YOUR NAME]`, `[WHAT YOU DO]`, `[THE AUDIENCE]` with your real details before you send. The more specific you are, the less generic the output. "A coach" gets you sludge. "A divorce-recovery coach for women in their 40s rebuilding after a 20-year marriage" gets you something with a pulse.

**Load context first.** The single biggest upgrade to any AI output is giving the model real material before you ask it to write. Paste in your old copy, a transcript of you talking, three things you've published, a client testimonial. Then prompt. A model with context writes like you; a model without context writes like the internet.

**Work in passes, not one shot.** Never accept the first draft. The prompts below are written to be run in sequence — generate, then critique, then tighten. The magic is in pass three, not pass one.

**Models.** These prompts are model-agnostic. They work in Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini, or anything else. For anything voice-sensitive — bios, posts, emails — Claude tends to hold a distinct voice longest. For raw ideation and volume, any frontier model is fine.

**The golden rule.** AI is your intern, not your author. It drafts. You decide. Never publish a sentence you would be embarrassed to have written yourself — because now you did.

## Capture Your Voice

Everything else in this vault depends on this section. Before you ask AI to write *as* you, you have to teach it what you sound like. This is the foundational move from The Byline Method — do it once, save the output, and paste it into the top of every future prompt.

**Prompt 1 — Build your voice profile**

```
You are a brand voice analyst. I'm going to paste in several samples of my
writing. Study them and return a "Voice Profile" I can reuse to brief AI tools.

Analyze:
- Sentence rhythm (long/short, fragments, punctuation habits)
- Vocabulary (words I reach for, words I'd never use)
- Tone (warm/blunt, formal/casual, earnest/wry)
- Structural tics (how I open, how I land a point, how I transition)
- The 5–7 rules a ghostwriter would need to sound exactly like me

Return it as a reusable brief, under 250 words, written as instructions to
another AI. Do not flatter me. Be specific enough that a stranger could fake me.

Here are the samples:
[PASTE 3–5 THINGS YOU'VE WRITTEN — POSTS, EMAILS, ANYTHING UNEDITED]
```

*When to use:* Once, at the start. Save the result as your "Voice Profile."
*Customize:* If you write in two registers (e.g. LinkedIn vs. client emails), run it twice and keep two profiles.

**Prompt 2 — Stress-test the profile**

```
Using the Voice Profile below, write three sentences about [ANY TOPIC].
Then tell me, honestly, which one is most likely to sound like AI wrote it,
and why. Rewrite that one to fix it.

Voice Profile:
[PASTE PROFILE FROM PROMPT 1]
```

*When to use:* Right after you build the profile, to verify it actually holds.

**Prompt 3 — The "talk it out" transcript**

```
I'm going to talk about [TOPIC] in a messy, unstructured way — this is a
transcript of me thinking out loud, not a draft. Don't clean up my voice.
Pull out the 5 sharpest, most original lines exactly as I said them, then
organize my raw thinking into an outline I could write from.

Transcript:
[PASTE A VOICE-MEMO TRANSCRIPT OR FREEWRITE]
```

*Customize:* This is the ghostwriter's secret — your spoken voice is usually better than your written one. Mine it.

## Positioning & Messaging

This is the Rewrite and New Chapter work: figuring out what you actually do and why it matters, in language a real person would use. AI is genuinely good here *if* you make it interrogate you instead of flatter you.

**Prompt 4 — The positioning interrogation**

```
Act as a sharp positioning strategist who is skeptical of buzzwords.
Interview me one question at a time (wait for each answer) to pin down my
positioning. Cover: who exactly I help, the specific problem I solve, what
they tried before me, what makes my approach different, and the transformation.
Push back when I'm vague or when I use jargon like "empower," "solutions,"
or "holistic." After 6–8 questions, give me a one-sentence positioning
statement a 12-year-old could understand.

Start with your first question.
```

*When to use:* When you can't explain what you do without a paragraph and a wince.

**Prompt 5 — The three-layer positioning statement**

```
Using everything I've told you, write my positioning in three layers:
1. The 7-word version (for a tagline)
2. The one-sentence version (for a bio line)
3. The one-paragraph version (for an about page)

Each layer must name a specific audience and a specific outcome. No "helping
people unlock potential." If you use a cliché, flag it and replace it.
```

**Prompt 6 — Find the line only you can say**

```
Here's how I currently describe what I do: [PASTE]. Here are three competitors:
[PASTE THEIR TAGLINES/ABOUT LINES]. Tell me which of my claims are
interchangeable with theirs (the parity claims) and which are genuinely mine.
Then propose three positioning angles that none of my competitors could
credibly steal. Rank them by how hard they'd be to copy.
```

*Customize:* The more competitor copy you paste, the better the differentiation.

## The Bio & LinkedIn About

The highest-leverage 200 words you own. This maps to Write Yourself Into the Room.

**Prompt 7 — The bio, three ways**

```
Write my professional bio in three lengths — a one-liner, a 50-word version,
and a 150-word version — using my Voice Profile and positioning below.

Rules:
- Lead with what I do for whom, not my job title
- One specific, concrete detail or result (no "passionate about")
- End the long version with something human, not a credential
- Banned words: passionate, results-driven, seasoned, guru, ninja, journey,
  leverage (as a verb), unlock, empower

Voice Profile: [PASTE]
Positioning: [PASTE]
Career facts: [PASTE — roles, results, the real story]
```

**Prompt 8 — The LinkedIn About rewrite**

```
Rewrite my LinkedIn "About" section. It should:
- Open with a hook in the first 2 lines (this is all people see before
  "see more" — make it impossible not to click)
- Be written in first person, like I'm talking to one person
- Have short paragraphs and white space, not a wall
- End with a clear, low-pressure call to action

Here's my current About (it's bad, that's why we're here): [PASTE]
Voice Profile: [PASTE]
```

**Prompt 9 — The hook generator**

```
Give me 10 first-line options for my bio/About that would make my exact
audience ([WHO]) stop scrolling. Range from plain-and-confident to slightly
provocative. No questions, no "Imagine if…", no fake vulnerability. After the
list, tell me which one is riskiest and which is safest.
```

## Website Copy

The Build, self-serve. Use AI to draft each page against a clear job — never "write my website" in one go.

**Prompt 10 — The homepage hero**

```
Write 5 homepage hero options (headline + subhead + button label) for [BRAND].
Each headline must pass this test: a stranger reads it and instantly knows
what I do and whether it's for them. No clever wordplay that hides the meaning.
The subhead earns the headline with one concrete specific. Button label is 2–4
words, active.

What I do: [PASTE]
Who it's for: [PASTE]
What makes it different: [PASTE]
```

**Prompt 11 — The page brief (run before writing any page)**

```
I'm about to write my [PAGE NAME] page. Before any copy, give me a one-page
brief: the single job of this page, the one action I want the visitor to take,
the 3 objections I must answer, the proof I need to show, and the order the
sections should go in. Be opinionated about the order.
```

**Prompt 12 — The About page (the one people actually read)**

```
Write my About page. Counter-intuitively, it's not about me — it's about why
I'm the right person for [AUDIENCE]'s problem. Structure: the problem they
have → why most solutions fail them → how I see it differently → who I am and
why I can be trusted → what to do next. Use my Voice Profile. Keep it human;
one real detail beats five adjectives.

Voice Profile: [PASTE]
My real story: [PASTE]
```

**Prompt 13 — CTA without the cringe**

```
Write 8 call-to-action options for [OFFER] that create momentum without
pressure tactics, fake scarcity, or "Don't miss out!!" Range from soft
("See how it works") to direct ("Book the call"). Match the tone to a
[CONFIDENT BUT WARM] brand.
```

## The Edit — Tighten Any Draft

The Edit, as a prompt. This is the highest-ROI section: run it on everything before you publish.

**Prompt 14 — The line edit**

```
Edit the text below for clarity and voice, not just grammar. Specifically:
- Cut every word that isn't working (aim to remove 15–25%)
- Replace abstractions with concrete specifics
- Kill hedging ("I think," "kind of," "just," "really")
- Vary sentence length so it has rhythm
- Keep MY voice — do not make it more "professional" or more polished if that
  sands off personality

Show the edited version, then a 3-bullet list of what you changed and why.

Text: [PASTE]
```

**Prompt 15 — The "does this sound like AI?" audit**

```
Read the text below and flag every sentence that sounds like it was written
by AI — the tells: hollow transitions ("In today's fast-paced world"),
tricolons everywhere, "it's not just X, it's Y," over-balanced sentences,
empty intensifiers, conclusions that restate the intro. Rewrite each flagged
sentence to sound like a specific human wrote it.

Text: [PASTE]
```

**Prompt 16 — The brutal cut**

```
This is [WORD COUNT] words. Cut it to [TARGET] without losing a single real
idea — only fat. Tell me what you cut and confirm nothing essential was lost.

Text: [PASTE]
```

## Content & Social

The Social Strategy Playbook in prompt form: pillars first, posts second, never the other way around.

**Prompt 17 — Content pillars from your actual expertise**

```
Based on my positioning and the topics below, propose 4 content pillars that
build authority for [WHO I WANT TO REACH]. For each pillar: the belief it
demonstrates, 5 example post angles, and the type of buyer it attracts. Avoid
generic pillars like "tips" or "motivation." Tie each to a real point of view
I hold.

Positioning: [PASTE]
Topics I know cold: [PASTE]
```

**Prompt 18 — The post, in your voice**

```
Write a LinkedIn post about [TOPIC / STORY]. Use my Voice Profile. Structure:
a hook that earns the first line, one idea developed with a specific example,
and a landing that gives the reader something to take away (not a generic
question). No hashtag soup, no "Agree?", no humble-brag disguised as a lesson.

Voice Profile: [PASTE]
The raw story or point: [PASTE]
```

**Prompt 19 — One idea, ten formats**

```
Take this single idea: [PASTE]. Repurpose it into: a short LinkedIn post, a
longer storytelling post, an X thread (5 posts), an email subject + first
line, a carousel outline (6 slides), and a one-line hook. Keep my voice
consistent across all of them.
```

**Prompt 20 — The 30-day calendar**

```
Build me a 30-day content calendar across my 4 pillars [PASTE PILLARS].
Mix formats (story, framework, contrarian take, behind-the-scenes, client
result). For each day: the pillar, the format, and a one-line angle. Don't
repeat angles. Front-load the strongest ideas.
```

## Email & Outreach

For nurture, launches, and the cold note that doesn't feel cold.

**Prompt 21 — The welcome email**

```
Write the first email someone gets after joining my list. It should: thank
them like a human (not a brand), set expectations for what they'll get,
deliver one piece of immediate value, and tell one short true thing about me
so they feel like they know a person. Voice Profile below. Keep it under
200 words.

Voice Profile: [PASTE]
What my list is about: [PASTE]
```

**Prompt 22 — The launch sequence skeleton**

```
Outline a 5-email launch sequence for [OFFER] opening [DATE] and closing
[DATE]. For each email: its single job, the emotional beat, the subject line,
and the one CTA. Map the arc from "here's a problem you have" to "last chance."
No false urgency — only real deadlines.

Offer details: [PASTE]
```

**Prompt 23 — Cold outreach that earns a reply**

```
Write 3 versions of a cold outreach message to [PERSONA] at [TYPE OF COMPANY].
Each must: open with something specific about THEM (not me), connect their
likely problem to what I do in one sentence, and ask for something small and
easy to say yes to. Under 90 words. No "I hope this email finds you well,"
no fake compliments, no paragraph about my company.

What I offer: [PASTE]
Who I'm writing to: [PASTE]
```

**Prompt 24 — Subject line lab**

```
Give me 15 subject lines for an email about [TOPIC], sorted into: curiosity,
benefit, and plain/direct. Then predict which 3 get the highest open rate for
a [DESCRIBE] audience and why. No clickbait that the email can't pay off.
```

## Offers, Pricing & Sales Pages

Turn the work into money without sounding like a late-night infomercial.

**Prompt 25 — Name and frame the offer**

```
Help me name and frame [OFFER]. Give me: 5 name options (clear beats clever),
a one-line promise, the transformation it delivers (before → after), what's
included as outcome-led bullets (not features), and the one-sentence reason
it's worth the price. Voice Profile below.

Voice Profile: [PASTE]
What the offer actually is: [PASTE]
Price: [PASTE]
```

**Prompt 26 — The sales page outline**

```
Outline a sales page for [OFFER] that earns trust before it asks for money.
Sections in order, each with its job: hook, the problem (in the reader's own
words), the cost of staying stuck, the offer, how it works, proof, who it's
for / not for, the guarantee, FAQ, final CTA. Flag where I'll need a
testimonial or a result to make it land.
```

**Prompt 27 — Objection handling**

```
List the top 8 reasons someone who needs [OFFER] would still not buy. For each
objection, write one honest sentence I could put on the page to address it
without being defensive or salesy.
```

## Strategy & Prep

Before the Session, as prompts. Use AI to think with you, not for you.

**Prompt 28 — The clarity audit**

```
Interview me to surface what I actually want from [PROJECT / QUARTER / LAUNCH].
Ask one question at a time. Dig past the first answer — when I say a goal,
ask why it matters and how I'd know it worked. After 6 questions, reflect back:
my real goal, the thing I'm avoiding, and the single highest-leverage next
action.
```

**Prompt 29 — The pre-mortem**

```
It's 6 months from now and [PROJECT] failed. Play three roles — a skeptical
customer, my future self, and a sharp advisor — and tell me why it failed.
Then give me the 3 things I should do now to prevent the most likely failure.
```

**Prompt 30 — The decision frame**

```
I'm deciding between [OPTION A] and [OPTION B]. Don't tell me what to do.
Instead, lay out the real trade-offs, name the assumption each option depends
on, and tell me what I'd have to believe for each to be the right call. End
with the one question that would break the tie.
```

## Guardrails — Keeping AI From Sounding Like AI

The difference between a brand that uses AI well and one that gets caught is editing. Keep these near your keyboard.

**The tells to delete on sight.** "In today's fast-paced world." "It's not just X, it's Y." "Let's dive in." "At the end of the day." "Game-changer." "Unlock / leverage / elevate / supercharge." Three-item lists in every sentence. Conclusions that just restate the opening. Em-dashes used as a verbal tic (says the guide full of em-dashes — use them on purpose).

**Prompt 31 — The de-slop pass**

```
Rewrite the text below to remove every AI tell: hollow openers, over-balanced
sentences, tricolons, empty intensifiers, and restated conclusions. Make it
sound like one specific, slightly opinionated human wrote it in one sitting.
Keep it the same length. Don't make it more formal — make it more alive.

Text: [PASTE]
```

**Prompt 32 — The read-aloud test**

```
Read this back to me the way I'd say it out loud. Mark any sentence I would
never actually say to a person's face, and rewrite it the way I'd really say it.

Text: [PASTE]
```
