## What's Inside

Seven complete guides — the DIY editions of the studio's core services.

### The Build Copy Guide

Every word you need for a new website — page by page, with fill-in templates, swipe copy, and the one test that separates copy that converts from copy that sounds nice. *Best for:* writing (or briefing) a full site without guessing.

### The Rewrite Playbook

Core messaging, overhauled. The framework for taking writing that's gone soft and making it land — so the results finally match the copy. *Best for:* when what you say no longer matches what you've become.

### The Social Strategy Playbook

A content system you'll actually use — pillars, voice calibration by platform, a 30-day batch sprint, and a posting rhythm that survives a bad week. *Best for:* showing up online with a system instead of relying on inspiration.

### The Byline Method

The ghostwriting framework for founder and executive thought leadership — how to sound like the smartest version of yourself, consistently, in public. *Best for:* building authority in writing without it eating your week.

### The Edit: DIY Edition

The copy-editing eye I bring to client work, translated into a framework you can run on your own bios, emails, LinkedIn, and landing pages. Audit, voice, line — in that order. *Best for:* tightening writing you've already drafted.

### The New Chapter Workbook

The strategy-session pre-work and positioning framework for a reinvention or relaunch — naming who you're becoming and how to say it. *Best for:* a pivot, a rebrand, or a return.

### Before the Session

The pre-work that turns any strategy session into something you actually use — the identity audit, the clarity questions, and the goal-setting frame that gets you to the real problem fast. *Best for:* getting maximum value from any consultant, or from yourself.

*…plus any future service guides. The Vault grows; your access doesn't expire.*

## Where to Start

By the job in front of you, not front to back:

- **"I'm building or relaunching a website."** → **The Build Copy Guide**, then **The Edit** to tighten every line.
- **"My messaging has gone generic."** → **The Rewrite Playbook**, then **The Edit.**
- **"I need to show up online consistently."** → **The Social Strategy Playbook**, then **The Byline Method** for the long-form authority layer.
- **"I'm reinventing or repositioning."** → **Before the Session** to surface the real problem, then **The New Chapter Workbook** to build the new position.

There's no wrong order — only the one you'll actually open.

## How to Use the Library

**Run one method to completion.** You own all seven, which makes it tempting to skim everything and ship nothing. Pick the job you actually have this month and do that guide fully. The rest will wait.

**Fill in the templates — don't just read them.** Every guide is built to be *used.* The brief, the snapshot, the checklists only work in ink. Reading them is research; filling them is the work.

**These pair with the writing library.** The Services Vault is the *craft* side; **The Vault** (the writing-and-identity ebooks) is the *voice and self* side. Most people need both — the method to execute and the voice to execute in.

> A framework you run beats a framework you admire. Open one. Start the first template.
