# Module 3 — The Voice: Sounding Like Yourself

*4 lessons · ~45 minutes · You'll leave with a documented brand voice that sounds like you and your faith — not a template.*

You now know what you're for (Module 2). This module is about *how you say it.* Because the fastest way to be ignored is to sound like everyone else who's ever made faith-based content. Your voice is your fingerprint. We're going to document it so it stops being an accident.

---

## Lesson 3.1 — Why faith content all sounds the same

Open any faith creator's feed and you'll hear the same dialect: gentle, vague, heavy on "friend" and "sweet friend," allergic to a strong claim, terrified of being too much. It's the verbal equivalent of beige.

It comes from a good place — humility, not wanting to seem arrogant about holy things. But beige doesn't read as humble. It reads as forgettable. And forgettable work can't serve anyone, because no one remembers it long enough to be served.

The women whose words actually stick — Zora Neale Hurston, the Psalmist, the prophets, the friend who tells you the truth at the kitchen table — share one trait: they *sound like a specific human being.* Specificity is the whole game. "God is faithful" is true and weightless. "God showed up the week the account hit $42 and I didn't tell anyone how scared I was" is true and *unforgettable.*

> Your voice is not a style you put on. It's the residue of telling the truth in your own words. Stop borrowing. Start excavating.

### Action step
Find three faith creators whose voices blur together. Write down the exact phrases that make them interchangeable. Now you know what to avoid.

---

## Lesson 3.2 — The tone spectrum

Voice isn't one setting. It's a position on several sliders. Map yours by placing an X on each:

```
Warm  ●────────────  Cool
Plain ────●────────  Lyrical
Gentle ──────●──────  Direct
Reverent ──●────────  Irreverent
Calm  ────────●────  Urgent
Polished ──────●────  Raw
```

There's no right position — only *your* position. The point is to choose on purpose. A voice that's warm + direct + raw lands completely differently than warm + lyrical + reverent. Both can be deeply faithful. They are not interchangeable, and pretending you have no position is how you end up beige.

A note for faith content specifically: you're allowed to be funny. You're allowed to be direct. You're allowed to have an edge. Reverence is about the heart, not the tone — and some of the most reverent people in Scripture were also the bluntest.

### Action step
Place yourself on all six sliders. Then write the one-line version: "My voice is ___, ___, and ___." Three adjectives, chosen on purpose.

---

## Lesson 3.3 — The do/don't vocabulary list

This is the single most practical voice tool there is, and almost no one builds it. Two columns: words and phrases you *use*, and words and phrases you *refuse.* It turns voice from a vibe into a checklist your future self (and any ghostwriter or VA) can follow.

| Words I use | Words I refuse |
| :-- | :-- |
| build, steward, the work, clarity, refuse, on purpose | sweet friend, hustle, manifest, crush it, just |
| name it, the gap, well, source | unlock your best self, game-changer, secret sauce |
| *(your specifics)* | *(your specifics)* |

The "refuse" column is where your voice gets its spine. Every banned word is a small act of resistance against sounding like the feed. Mine includes "just" (it apologizes) and "content" when I mean "the work." Yours will be different — that's the point.

### Action step
Build your two columns. Minimum ten words each. Pull the "use" words from your Source File — your real study language is your most honest vocabulary.

---

## Lesson 3.4 — The voice document

Now assemble it into one page you'll actually reference. Your Voice Document has five parts:

1. **The three adjectives** (from 3.2).
2. **The tone sliders**, marked.
3. **The do/don't vocabulary** (from 3.3).
4. **Two or three sentences of "sounds like me"** — actual lines you've written that feel unmistakably yours. Your north stars.
5. **One "before/after"** — a beige sentence rewritten in your voice, so the difference is visible.

Example of part 5:
> **Before:** "I'm so excited to share some tips to help you grow in your faith journey, sweet friends!"
> **After:** "Most study habits fail for one reason: no method. Here's the one I use — and why it survives a hard week."

That contrast *is* your brand. Keep the document one page. A voice guide nobody reads protects nothing.

### Action step
Build your one-page Voice Document with all five parts. Save it next to your calling statement. From here on, everything you publish gets checked against it.
